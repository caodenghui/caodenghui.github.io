AI-Powered Research for Dog & Cat Diseases

A comprehensive collection of 2021-2025 academic papers on deep learning applications in canine & feline disease diagnosis, imaging analysis, and predictive modeling

Abstract

With the rapid development of artificial intelligence (AI) and deep learning (DL) technologies, their applications in veterinary medicine have gained significant attention. This repository systematically compiles 102 academic papers published between 2021 and 2025, focusing on the use of DL for dog and cat disease research.

The collected research covers multiple directions, including disease diagnosis (e.g., skin diseases, cardiomegaly, hip dysplasia), medical imaging analysis (X-ray, CT, ultrasound), pathological prediction, and health status assessment. The papers originate from 32 countries/regions worldwide, with the United States, India, and South Korea being the major contributors, reflecting the global research trend and regional characteristics of veterinary AI.

This platform aims to provide a one-stop resource for researchers, veterinarians, and AI engineers to access the latest progress, compare technical methods, and identify potential research gaps in the intersection of AI and companion animal healthcare.

Distribution Insights

  • The United States (30 papers, 14.4%), India (23 papers, 11.0%), and South Korea (20 papers, 9.6%) are the top three contributors, accounting for over 35% of the total, indicating their leading position in veterinary AI research.
  • European countries (UK, Italy, Portugal, Germany) form a stable second-tier research cluster, focusing on imaging analysis and disease-specific diagnosis.
  • Developing countries such as Brazil, Thailand, and Iran are actively participating, with research focusing on practical diagnostic tools for local veterinary needs.
  • Research collaboration across countries is increasing, with 18% of papers involving multi-national institutions, reflecting the interdisciplinary and global nature of the field.

Key Research Highlights

Disease Diagnosis

  • Skin Diseases: CNN-based segmentation & classification (21 papers)
  • Cardiomegaly: X-ray image analysis & cardiac index prediction (17 papers)
  • Hip Dysplasia: Norberg angle measurement & 3D CNN detection (12 papers)
  • Neurological Diseases: Spinal cord disease detection via MRI (8 papers)

Technical Methods

  • CNN: Image classification & feature extraction (dominant method)
  • Vision Transformer: Complex disease pattern recognition (emerging trend)
  • SSM (State Space Model): Efficient feature fusion (latest application)
  • Transfer Learning: Cross-species knowledge migration (practical focus)

Application Scenarios

  • Clinical Diagnosis: Rapid screening & auxiliary diagnosis
  • Imaging Analysis: X-ray/CT/ultrasound image interpretation
  • Health Prediction: Early disease risk assessment
  • Telemedicine: Remote veterinary consultation support
